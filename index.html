About Dr. Zarinebaf

Fariba Zarinebaf obtained her B.A. from the University of Illinois at Chicago. She received her Ph.D. in Middle Eastern and Islamic history from the University of Chicago. Her research and publications focus on the urban, social, gender, and legal history of the Ottoman empire and Iran from the early modern to the modern periods. She also teaches and does research on early modern empires with a focus on borderlands, port cities, caravan towns and rural landscapes.

She has published on the Ottoman- Iranian borderland (Azerbaijan) as well as the Venetian- Ottoman borderland in Greece during the eighteenth century. She has also published on the the history of Iranian diaspora in Istanbul, comparative history of constitutionalism in Iran and the Ottoman Empire as well as gender and law in the pre-modern Middle East.
